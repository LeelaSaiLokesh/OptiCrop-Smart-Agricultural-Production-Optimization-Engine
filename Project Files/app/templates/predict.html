Measurement Guide

How to Measure Parameters

Accurate measurements ensure the most reliable AI crop recommendations. Follow these best practices.

🧪

How to Measure NPK

  • Collect soil from 10–15 spots at 0–15 cm depth
  • Mix samples and take 500g composite sample
  • Send to certified soil testing lab or use the Soil Health Card scheme
  • Use digital NPK soil meters for quick field readings
  • Record values in mg/kg (ppm) as reported by the lab
🧫

How to Measure pH

  • Mix 1 part soil with 2.5 parts distilled water
  • Allow suspension to settle for 30 minutes
  • Calibrate pH meter with buffer solutions (4.0 and 7.0)
  • Insert probe and record stable reading
  • pH strips are acceptable but less accurate than meters
🌡️

Temperature & Humidity

  • Use mean annual temperature from IMD station data
  • Download district-level data from data.gov.in
  • Use a digital thermo-hygrometer for local readings
  • OpenWeatherMap API provides historical averages
  • Avoid using extreme single-day readings
⚠️

Common Mistakes

  • Using old soil test reports (more than 2 years old)
  • Collecting samples only from the surface layer
  • Mixing samples from different field zones
  • Using rainfall from a different season or year
  • Entering temperature in Fahrenheit instead of Celsius

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the AI crop prediction engine.

Your 7 soil and climate parameters are submitted to the Flask backend. The inputs are normalized using StandardScaler (fitted on training data) and fed into a Random Forest classifier (300 decision trees) trained on the Kaggle Crop Recommendation Dataset (2,200 records, 22 crops). The model computes class probabilities for all 22 crops and returns the crop with the highest probability along with its confidence score. Inference completes in under 50ms.

N (Nitrogen) drives vegetative growth — it's essential for leaf and stem development. P (Phosphorus) supports root development, flowering, and fruiting. K (Potassium) regulates water uptake, disease resistance, and fruit quality. All three are the primary macronutrients measured in soil tests and reported in mg/kg (ppm). Soil Health Cards issued by the Indian government provide these values for registered farmland.

The production model (Random Forest, 300 estimators) achieves 99.5% accuracy on the held-out test set (20% of 2,200 records). This was validated through 10-fold stratified cross-validation. F1-Score, Precision, Recall, and ROC-AUC are all above 0.99. The accuracy may vary if your soil parameters fall outside the training distribution (rare soils, extreme climates) — in such cases, treat the prediction as advisory rather than definitive.

The model supports common Indian staple crops, cash crops, fruits, and pulses.
